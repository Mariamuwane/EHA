My Favourite Recipe

My favourite recipe is Ofada rice and it's sauce.
It is a Yoruba dish and quite popular in the Southern part of Nigeria.
It is nutritious and I find it fun to devour.
I will share below the preparation guide from The Guardian.

Table of Ingredients

Item Number
Green Bell Peppers (Green Tatashe) 6
Scotch bonnet (Ata Rodo) 3-4
Big Red Onion Chopped 1
Palm Oil ( Not specified because you’ll be frying the meats with it as well) Unspecified
Cup Chicken or Beef Stock (optional) 1
Cups Boiled Assorted Meats( cut into bite size chunks) 4
Tablespoons Locust Beans (Iru) 2
Cup Shredded Boiled Stockfish (Panla) 1
Tablespoons Dried Crayfish 2
knorr Chicken Cubes or your preferred bullion cube 2
Salt Unspecified

Before you begin

  • PREP! PREP! PREP!!
  • If you’ll be roasting your peppers, do so by placing them on a foil tray or roasting
    tray, place in a preheated oven, 200 degrees and roast them for about 45 minutes.
    When they’re roasted, blend coarsely and set aside.
  • Place a big pot on a hob on medium heat, pour in palm oil, enough quantity to fry
    your meats. Leave to bleach for 15-10 minutes thereabouts, when the oil is bleached,
    pour in your meats and fry till slightly crisp. When they’re all fried, scoop out the
    meat and side aside.
  • Wash and dice the beef, goat meat and optional tripe (shaki) and (cow skin)
    ponmon into small pieces .Place the meat in a medium sized pan, slice and add in
    ½ an onion, salt, 2 stock cube(Maggi) and 1 chilli pepper.Cook the meat for 30
    minutes until tender

Cooking Directions

  1. Wash the peppers and peel the onions.
  2. Blend the peppers and onions in a Food Processor or Food Blender.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a Pot and once hot add the Palm oil.
  4. Add the salt and Maggi cubes.
  5. Add the Stock fish, cover pot and cook for about 5 minutes to soften.
  6. Add the meats and stir them into the stew.
  7. Add Iru/Locust beans.
  8. Cover pot and cook for a further 10 minutes.
  9. Turn down the heat and allow the sauce to simmer and a little water ,preferably meat stock instead of water.
  10. Serve with white rice or Ewa/Beans and rice.
